The Story of Ayyuush

“Broken bones. Unbroken spirit.”

Real name Arif, but the world calls him Ayyuush. From the beautiful land of Assam, India, this young soul carries dreams far greater than himself. A student of Class 11 Science, with a heart full of courage — he’s walking a path that connects both logic and justice.

He dreams of wearing the uniform of a Police Officer, standing for truth. And at the same time, mastering the world of cyber knowledge — becoming a White Cap Hacker. His mission: to protect people, both on ground and online.

But the story wasn’t easy. Back in Class 2, a small playful push from a friend broke both his hands. No anger, no hate — only pain, and a stronger will. Again in Class 4, while washing mangoes outside, he slipped — and the same hands broke again. Yet he smiled through the pain.

His school, Sankar Ajan Jatiya Vidyalaya, gave him more than just education — it gave him a family of twelve.

The 12 Diamonds: Muzahid, Mumin, Arsaful, Sidhartha, Debojyuti, Prodyut, Tanushree, Sibani, Punima, Susmita, Arfin — and Ayyuush himself. Together, they created memories that time can’t erase.

Life tested him — weak eyesight, broken hands, and challenges many would give up on. But not him. His heart beats with a fire: “Main gir gaya tha… par kabhi ruk nahi gaya.”

One dream keeps him alive — to meet MS Dhoni once before he leaves this world. Because like Dhoni, he too believes that calmness is power.

From a small town boy to a rising dreamer — this is not just a story. It’s a message to everyone who ever felt broken:

“Zindagi haath tod sakti hai… par sapne nahi.”
